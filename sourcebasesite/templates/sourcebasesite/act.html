Electric vehicles are better than fossil fuel vehicles

    PerspectiveWriters' Votes
    3

    EV charging infrastructure does not support carefree motoring, where as the fossil fuel supply network is mature and ubiquitous.

    Pros
    Cons

    • All the EV charging networks are "on their way" but not ready yet. Fossil fuel charging networks are well established and reliable.

    • Fossil fuel cars have far greater range than EVs so you don't have to fill up so often, making the driving experience much more carefree.

    • When filling up with fossil fuel you can just pay by credit card. With EVs you need a variety of apps and RFID cards that you have to prepare in advance in order to be able to charge.

    • Fossil fuels are readily available pretty much anywhere you go. Drivers don't need to plan their journey to take into account where they will re-fuel as much as you do with an EV. With an EV you have to plan in advance where you might charge.

    • Electrical power outlets are more ubiquitous than gasoline fueling stations.
      Mobile chargers.

    • The Tesla Supercharger and Destination Charger network proves that carefree motoring is possible. Long road trips are easy and simple to do with no pre-planning. Once other charger networks get up to the same standard all EVs will have carefree motoring.

    • In rural areas, lack of gasoline availability can impede carefree motoring just as easily. In many of these places, RV parks have available power to charge electric vehicles.

    • The UK has 8400 Fuel Stations and 7300 EV Charging Points; the infrastructure is there already and it's growing daily. Surely, this means we have reached the critical mass, infrastructure-wise.
      Statista: UK fuel stations, 2016

    • A variety of charging options are being installed in most countries, ranging from rapid chargers on main highways, top up chargers at supermarkets, and day chargers at places where people work.
